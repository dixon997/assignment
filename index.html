Spend a perfect day in Coney Island

History of Coney Island:

Coney Island, also known as Pulau Serangoon, is located at the coast of Punggol in the northeastern region of Singapore. In 1950, it was sold to a businessman who developed it into a resort. This however, only lasted for 3 years. In 1975 the government acquired ownership to the island and began land reclamation to expand the island. On 10 October 2015, Coney Island Park was officially opened to the public.


Things To Do:

Cycle

Many people choose to come to this island to cycle around and look at the scenery. You may choose to follow the 2.4km trail or follow the signs that will lead you to 5 beach areas. If you don't have a bicycle, you can rent one from Jomando Adventure & Recreations or Go Cycling at Punggol Settlement.

Bird Watch

There are over 80 species of birds on the island. Take the opportunity at the Bird Hide to take photographs of them in their natural habitat.

Getting There:

By Public Transport

Take the train to Punggol MRT station and walk towards the bus interchange through exit C. Take bus 84 from the Punggol interchange and alight at Punggol Road End (Punggol Point Park). Walk about 500 m east (past the restaurants) along the Punggol Promenade Punggol Point Walk to reach the starting point at the West Entrance of Coney Island Park.

By Car

Drive to Punggol Settlement and park at the outdoor public carpark. Walk 500 m east (past the restaurants) along Punggol Promenade Punggol Point Walk to reach the starting point at the West Entrance of Coney Island Park.
